Our Departments

The Northview FAA proudly supports Art, Band, Chorus, Drama, and Orchestra — five distinct departments serving over 900 student artists.

Art

Northview's Art program gives students the skills and space to develop their creative voice across drawing, painting, sculpture, digital art, and more. Students learn foundational techniques, explore art history, and build a portfolio worthy of collegiate review.

How the FAA Helps

FAA funds art supplies, display materials for student exhibitions, entry fees for juried art shows, and equipment upgrades that expand what students can create.

Highlights

  • State-recognized student portfolio submissions
  • Annual spring art exhibition open to families
  • Advanced Placement Art courses available

Band

From Marching Band to Wind Ensemble, Northview's Band program is a year-round commitment that builds discipline, teamwork, and musical excellence. Students perform at football games, competitions, and formal concerts throughout the school year.

How the FAA Helps

FAA funds instrument purchases and repairs, uniform upkeep and replacements, travel and registration fees for competitions, and equipment for the percussion section.

Highlights

  • Superior ratings at LGPE (Large Group Performance Evaluation)
  • State Marching Band competition participant
  • Multiple Honor Band students annually

Chorus

Northview Chorus develops singers across multiple ensembles including Concert Choir, Women's Chorus, and Select Choir. Students grow in vocal technique, music theory, and performance, competing regionally and performing at events throughout Georgia.

How the FAA Helps

FAA funds sheet music, performance attire, competition registration and travel, and the annual Spring Concert production costs.

Highlights

  • Superior ratings at LGPE
  • Students selected for All-State Chorus
  • Community performance partnerships

Drama

Northview Drama produces full-scale theatrical productions with student actors, directors, crew, and designers. Students learn acting technique, stagecraft, lighting and sound design, and the collaborative discipline of live theatre — culminating in performances that fill the Northview auditorium every season.

How the FAA Helps

FAA funds costume design and construction, set building materials, lighting and sound equipment, royalty fees for scripts, and production promotion.

Highlights

  • Fall play and spring musical each year
  • State Theatre competition entries
  • Technical theatre and design track available

Orchestra

Northview Orchestra offers string players a path from beginner to advanced through multiple ensembles. Students develop technical proficiency and expressive musicianship, performing in formal concerts and competing at state-level events throughout the year.

How the FAA Helps

FAA funds bow rehairs, instrument repairs (including school-owned cellos and basses), music purchases, competition travel, and accessories for students in need.

Highlights

  • Superior ratings at LGPE
  • Students selected for All-State Orchestra
  • Chamber ensemble opportunities for advanced students
